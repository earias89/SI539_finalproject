Research Projects

Voluntary Childlessness Over Time in the U.S.

Institute for Social Research at University of Michigan, Ann Arbor

I am currently using population representative data to measure the change in proportion of voluntarily childless people over time and sociodemographic predictors of voluntary childlessness. This project will additonally examine reciprocal relationships between fertility intentions, health, and the relationship experiences among men and women.

Evaluating the Accessibility and Usability of the Michigan Safer Opioid Prescribing Toolkit

School of Nursing and the Injury Prevention Center at University of Michigan, Ann Arbor

A combination of UX Research methods were employed to (1) measure the social demographics most strongly associated with toolkit use and common uses; (2) identify user goals, and the extent this resource fulfills these goals; and (3) whether users of the toolkit consider this a “one-stop-shop” resource. User attitudes about the usability and accessibility were additionally measured.

IdealMate: Dating for Nontraditional Communties

School of Information at University of Michigan, Ann Arbor

I designed IdealMate to meet the needs of voluntarily childfree, involuntarily childfree, non-monogamous, and asexual dating app users using a combination of user interviews, personas, storyboards, userflows, hi and lo-fidelity prototype testing, wireframing, and usability testing.

Binding Research to Advance Transmasculine Health (BREATHE) Study

School of Nursing and the Injury Prevention Center at University of Michigan, Ann Arbor

I reviewed interview transcripts, compiled a list of interview quotes, and conducted a thematic analysis that was used to visually represent transmasculine binding experiences. These depictions were then used for a health education comic book.

Enhanced Assess, Acknowledge, and Act (EAAA) Sexual Assault Resistance Education Program

School of Nursing and the Injury Prevention Center at University of Michigan, Ann Arbor

I reviewed interview transcripts from first-year undergraduate students on their perceptions of online-based sexual assault prevention programs, and used the findings to design an online program version of the EAAA education program.

Chitwan Valley Family Study (CVFS)

Institute for Social Research at University of Michigan, Ann Arbor

I disaggregated data to analyze patterns of social change and family formation over time, and assisted with preparing research papers for publication. I also worked on the backend of the CVFS website to ensure all publications and information match the publisher website.

Longitudinal Childfree Study

Client: Laura Carroll, Nonfiction Author & Expert on the Childfree Choice

Analyzed qualitative data using NVivo software for a longitudinal study on childfree women.

Cultural Consensus of Love Experiences

I assisted with survey design & scale selection to examine the cultural consensus of felt love among men and women in the U.S. The data collected from this survey included measures of sexual attraction and identity, love languages, relationship styles, & Big Five Personality traits.

Casual Sex Study; Orgasm Goal Orientation Study

Summer Research Opportunity Program (SROP) Scholar in the Psychology department at University of Michigan, Ann Arbor

I conducted content analyses of The Journal of Social and Personal Relationships and Personal Relationships and identitfed studies that examined physical and instrumental reasons for why men and women engage in casual sex. I also presented on casual sex differences between men and women at the final SROP poster symposium

Consensual Non-Monogamy Pilot Study

I recruited and interviewed couples about the benefits and challenges of committing to an alternative relationship style. Participants were asked to create a historical timeline using color-coded caption cards to pair significant events with associated emotions experienced throughout the course of their primary relationship. I also analyzed and coded interview transcripts, and presented at the 2016 Western Psychological Association conference.

Social Media as a Relationship Outlet

I conducted an independent study and deisgned an online survey that sought to investigate relationships between online extra-dyadic interaction, relationship commitment, & social expansion among socially monogamous people.

Mate Preference Shifts and Women's Ovulation Cycles

I prescreened participants for eligibility, collected participant consent forms, & proctored in-person survey data collection. This research supported an original UCLA study directed by Dr. Elizabeth Pillsworth & Dr. Martie Haselton that examined women's ovulation shifts, extra-pair attraction, and mate retention behavior.
